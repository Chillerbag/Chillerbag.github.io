currently reading

The Accursed Share from Georges Bataille. It's a bit of a mindfuck that puts into perspective how much economic principles in their seemingly extrinsic nature are deep rooted in a understanding of the "totality" of humanity. I'm up to vol 3. In vol 1 Bataille is concerned with defining the nature of transactions in all function, by which our constant excess energy demands glorious waste. Also defines what production is "useful" (of which there is not much). In vol 2 eroticism becomes the vessel by which we delve deeper into humanity, specifcally, how it commands a reflection of our animal nature and concluding that our horror of our animal origins is a necessary indulgence, but in erotic moments, the gaze into the animal components is what truly makes us human.
