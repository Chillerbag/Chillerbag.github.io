This weekend, I have to start a project from scratch for work. The motivation required is immense, because the idea is bad and boring and generally bad for society. In any case, like anyone, I struggle and stall in actually starting anything. Which poses real risk for two of the projects sitting in my head these days, where someone could really beat me to the punch on both of them. It doesn’t come down to a lack of motivation really, but a distracted and racing mind. One that wants relentlessly without the transitory state of working to realise those wants. To be seen as something other than a productive force desperately, and one that yearns for like minded collaborators, but finds a general apathy in my friends. For the latter, I feel as though I need to make something on my own in order to meet people, I suppose, but my body and time wasted in trying to sum the motivation to work does not will it so, in a spiral of unproductivity and isolation. It could be solved by working some magical game development job or something, but I don’t grind Leetcode because I really struggle to sum the motivation for something so banal and bureaucratic. The idea of failing a Leetcode problem too is so damaging to my fragile ego as a “software engineer” that I think it would drive me toward depression, feeling as though I have somehow gamed the system to be an engineer, and am living a vile lie. Being seen as someone who *can* create art seems to be godhood to me, the same way creating good code is, in my realised imposter syndrome, where I envy all those around me and find film screenings unbearable in their actualisation in the impossible distance between me and a director. Art and the consumption of it seems to be the reason for my continued existence, especially as a corporate worker whose life can be categorised largely as a string of insignificance. The problem is, that my drive to create without love of creation does superimpose a productive entity upon me, despite my desire to escape it.
<br></br>
I read a good article from a trans woman a long time ago I have since lost, which reflected on what art can communicate from a class perspective, and essentially that our cultural experience is so unified through homogeneous algorithmic consumption, that new art is distilled into class, which tends toward rich white men art. In that case, it’s perhaps possible that I have no unique life experience with which to offer, as I have lived a very coddled life, and I largely seem to not experience anything at all. In fact, the one short film script I have written largely deals with some distortion of me hooking up with a series of women and lying to them about the movies the character has seen, I.e, the perception of sexual partners as a kind of game or task. It’s a little more complex than that, but it doesn’t seem like I’ll know enough people to really film it any time soon. I could probably reach out to some people though. But I guess the point is an additional excuse from mine is that what I make couldn’t have value, though I guess that isn’t the point of creation. Things only have value in retrospect. 
<br></br>
I try to work from the library and begin my day in some kind of way which purifies my mind, like reading a book outside or drinking coffee on the couch without stimulation or input. The reason the library allows me to start is out of a sense of humiliation, that these people are doing something where I am not. They have begun, they have crossed that barrier, and as such it must be effortless as they relentlessly churn through their writing and drawing and whatever. Further to this, I have to confront the fact that I do not know what I am doing, that I simply, am stupid. I am not a profound software engineer who can simply make the thing, I need to skim 1000 pages to piece any idea together. I have to scratch my head like a hapless fool in front of the fellow library visitors. It’s kind of exposure therapy, so I can’t be morose and give up at my own stupidity. Really, even in university the library served as the place where I could take advantage of my burning need to be accepted as someone who was productive, since everyone there was, and if I weren’t it looked as though I was an academic failure. 
<br></br>
There must be some way to start that isn’t harmonising with extremely negative emotions, some way to accept the time you spend watching a movie that sucks or playing a game that you didn’t make any progress thing as an acceptable use of time, and for breaks like that to become a meaningful part of your productivity. Currently, my move is to relentlessly slam my head into a brick wall, despair at any “break” which makes them unfulfilling, and repeat until total loss of focus and burnout, but some kind of homunculus product at the end. Slow down, I guess, but I don’t believe that.
