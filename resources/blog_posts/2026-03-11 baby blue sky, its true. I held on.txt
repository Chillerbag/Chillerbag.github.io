Do you ever think that one day, we'll be too busy. Maybe it'll be warm yet again, a burgeoning second summer without you. As I get older, I realise all the summers I categorise as belonging to myself, I shared with you. We'll be too busy and we'll bump into each other. The narratives spun over the months-long gaps in contact will disintegrate. Our plans will be destroyed; we'll be seeing the world at an angle all day. All balance will disappear. We will feel a mix of joy and reopened wounds. We'll look different, painted colors of new experience, nigh unrecognisable inward and outward. This isn't the person you containerised within your head as love, as whatever it was to you. We will make a joke about not knowing where to begin. Awkward facial expressions will light a familiar candle under new ceilings.
<br></br>
I realise, now, as the city passes through beams above the platform and people shuffle on, stumbling through their daily routine, that you will never be the same person again. And despite this, despite all that you could become and have ever been, I still love you. I'll always fall in love with you, whoever that is. 
<br></br>
But the city is lively tonight, I can't be sad. It's cheering in the distance for no particular event. Its getting cold again, and people will retreat from it in hibernation. I am a distant observer, seeing its life in shadows darting across the light in bunches, with two shadows trailing behind forming one coherent blob. Of imaginary first meetings in bars, and reunions in new suburbs. It's lively tonight, and I hope you're out there, or who you have become is out there, smiling. Laughing with those that you now love.
